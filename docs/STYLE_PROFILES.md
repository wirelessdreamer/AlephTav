# Style Profiles

Current bundled profiles:

- `formal_liturgical`
- `study_literal`
- `dynamic_equivalent`
- `metered_common_meter`
- `performative_free`
- `source_imagist`
- `doubter_lament`

Profiles define literalness, lyric freedom, target syllables, rhyme mode, register, and parallelism priority.
They also define how creative layers stay anchored to Hebrew while still sounding alive in English.

Additional profile axes:

- `source_anchor_mode`: whether outputs stay token-close, preserve the Hebrew image-world, or preserve the broader scene while reordering more freely.
- `metaphor_mode`: whether artistic passes should minimize added symbolism, keep source metaphors first, or allow contemporary symbolic equivalents.
- `imagery_preservation`: how strongly the generator should retain concrete source images instead of flattening them into abstract emotion.
- `idiom_modernity`: how aggressively the generator should avoid inherited Bible English in favor of contemporary diction.
- `emotional_directness`: how exposed and immediate the speaker should sound.
- `divine_name_rendering`: whether the profile should preserve the marked divine name, prefer a contemporary title such as `Lord`, or allow a reader-facing `God`/`my God` alternate in direct address.
- `faith_posture`: whether the voice should read as confessional, observational, or contested/doubting.

Recommended use:

- `study_literal`: Hebrew-first study and audit work.
- `source_imagist`: poetic English that keeps the source images plainly visible.
- `dynamic_equivalent`: natural modern phrasing without strong lyric compression.
- `metered_common_meter`: singable lines with tighter syllable goals.
- `performative_free`: contemporary spoken cadence with more compression.
- `doubter_lament`: intimate, emotionally direct, contested-faith wording that still stays anchored to Hebrew imagery.
