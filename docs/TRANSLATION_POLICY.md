# Translation Policy

- Hebrew canonical source is UXLC/WLC-derived only.
- OSHB and MACULA may enrich lexical and linguistic metadata.
- Sefaria witnesses are optional, version-pinned, and separate from canonical source.
- IDs are immutable and independent of text status.
- Canonical promotion always requires human review.
- Alternate renderings remain first-class and exportable by policy selection.
- Phrase, concept, lyric, and metered lyric layers must translate from Hebrew token data and locked upstream layers, not from English witness text.
- Artistic rewrites must preserve speaker/addressee, scene logic, and source-image pressure before abstracting into generalized emotion.
- Contemporary symbolic language is allowed only when the Hebrew image-world remains legible and the concession is surfaced in rationale or drift flags.
- Style profiles may target confessional, observational, or contested faith posture; the generator must not auto-strengthen doubtful speech into devotional certainty.
